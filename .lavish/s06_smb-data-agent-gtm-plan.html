GTM wedge and demo-data roadmap

Bring enterprise-grade insight workflows to SMB operators

The strongest positioning is not “chat with any database.” It is a governed insight workspace that turns messy SMB data into answers, charts, repeatable reports, and client-ready narratives. The demo strategy should prove a painful business outcome before asking for private data.

Recommendation

Start with a property intelligence wedge because your current rent and sale data already supports credible demos, location-specific outreach, and report outputs that agents, buyer's agents, property managers, and small developers understand immediately.

Build the product so the demo story generalizes: “connect your finance, sales, marketing, and ops data; ask questions; generate reports; keep access governed.” Then add finance and ecommerce demo packs to prove the platform is not locked to property.

Positioning to test

Your SMB data analyst, report builder, and governed SQL layer in one place.

The buyer does not want a database chatbot. They want “tell me what changed, why it matters, and send me a polished report I can act on or forward.”

Risks and guardrails

Risk Why it matters Mitigation
“Any data” positioning is too broad SMB buyers buy a business outcome, not a general analytics platform. Sell one vertical workflow first; keep the architecture generic under the surface.
Personalized demos can feel invasive Prospects may object if the demo appears to use private data without permission. Label data source clearly: public market data, synthetic data, or prospect-supplied export.
Data cleanup kills pilots Messy CSVs and inconsistent accounting categories can consume all delivery time. Create strict import templates, sample schemas, profiling checks, and a “first 3 questions” onboarding path.
AI trust gap SMBs may like the demo but hesitate to trust generated answers. Expose SQL, audit trail, source tables, chart data, and report citations. Keep actions human-approved.
