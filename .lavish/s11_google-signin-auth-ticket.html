The key insight: it's the Entra path, re-pointed

services/backend-api/app/auth.py already does exactly what Google needs — just for Microsoft: an EntraVerifier that loads OIDC metadata, validates the token against the provider's JWKS with audience + issuer, then _provision_entra_user() JIT-upserts the identity into app.users. Google is a standard OIDC provider, so this becomes a GoogleVerifier pointed at Google's endpoints.

Risks & notes

Consent screen: testing vs published

In "testing" mode only allowlisted Google accounts can sign in (fine for a demo). Publishing to all external users needs Google's verification. Decide who logs in.

Trust the email_verified claim

Provision on sub (immutable), not email; only treat email as trusted when email_verified is true. Prevents email-spoof edge cases.

Admin mapping differs from Entra

Entra used app-roles in the token; Google won't. Use a settings admin_emails allowlist (simple, env-configurable) instead.

Redirect URIs are per-environment

Localhost now; the cloud CloudFront URL is added in s12 once it exists. Same client ID can carry multiple origins.
